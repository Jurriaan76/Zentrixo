# Brand Research Builder - Prompts

These are the exact system prompts you must use when executing each step of the Brand Research Builder skill.

## Step 1: Product Explanation
**Prompt to use:**
"Give me an explanation of what this product actually does, what the functionalities are, and what the benefits are. Write it as simply as possible so that anyone reading this description immediately understands what the product is. 
Product info provided by user: [INSERT PRODUCT INFO/LINK/IMAGE DESCRIPTION]"

## Step 2: Market Research
**Prompt to use:**
"Today you're going to be an expert marketeer as well as an expert on human psychology & biology. We're going to be analyzing and researching the market for people who are interested in purchasing our product: [PRODUCT NAME]
[SHORT DESCRIPTION OF THE PRODUCT]
This is an example link of a similar product on Amazon as reference: [LINK TO AMAZON IF PROVIDED]

I need you to research the customers who are interested in buying our product:
- Psychographic Profile
- Emotions needed to evoke a purchase
- Lifestyle choices
- Purchasing decision making logic
- Main desire for purchasing the product
- Main problems needed to be solved
- Biological reasons for purchasing the product

Also, analyze what actual customers say about this product. Tell me who those customers are, why they purchased the product, what they use it for, what they like about the product and what they don’t like about the product. Research all the places you can think of. Like Amazon reviews, different forums and Reddit.
Start Research"

## Step 3: Customer Avatars
**Prompt to use:**
"Based on the research you’ve done, we need to come up with a customer persona where we are going to focus on. We need to define an audience where we can market to. So that this audience experiences the ''this product is made for me!'' feeling. Instead of generic marketing. Make sure to use all the research you’ve done in order to come up with these audiences. We want to evoke the right emotions and use their deepest desires.

I want you to come up with a list of 10 different audiences where we can focus on. With each audience, please list the following: 
- Short description of this audience 
- Their emotional desire related to this product 
- Main problem(s) we are solving with the product 
- Main marketing angle / promise (ex. ''The [product name] helps students be more creative, improves memory and boost the average results with 32%'')"

## Step 4: Customer Avatar - Deep Dive
**Prompt to use:**
"Work as a first-class marketing analyst with deep insight into the real psychology of buyers. Your task is to uncover the true emotional drivers behind why our ideal customer would buy. Not the socially acceptable reasons, but the raw, unfiltered truth. Most people buy based on emotion first, and only then on logic — so we need the deep fears, secret desires, internal conflicts, and external pressures that influence their decisions.

You will build a detailed buyer personality that goes far beyond superficial data. Your insights are only used to guide prospects toward solutions that are in their own best interest. You do not need to censor uncomfortable truths — this is private and will never be shown to the prospect.

Make sure to include all research you have done. Follow these steps:

Build a complete buyer profile for [INSERT CHOSEN PERSONA OR "BRAND-WIDE"] with the following structure:
• Demographic Info: Name, age, short summary.
• Core Problem: Their dominant pain point.
• Top 5 Emotional Triggers: Emotions tied to that core problem.
• Top 5 Deep Fears: What keeps them up at night.
• How Fears Affect Relationships: Specific effects on partners, children, friends, colleagues, etc.
• Painful Quotes From Others: Judgments, even from “supportive” people.
• Failed Past Solutions: 5 attempts, with fragments of internal monologue.
• What They Refuse to Do: Tasks, sacrifices, or risks they are unwilling to take.
• Dream Transformation: What the perfect solution looks and feels like.
• Post-Transformation Quotes: What others would say about their new self.
• Market Psychology: What they believe success depends on; what they would need to give up emotionally to change; who/what they blame for their problem; top 5 objections holding them back.

Tone: Use the language of our prospect. Keep it real, raw, emotional, and specific — as if we’re eavesdropping on their private thoughts or hearing honest conversations with trusted friends."

## Step 5: Brand Guidelines
**Prompt to use:**
"Create a comprehensive Brand Identity Guidelines document for a high-quality consumer brand. Make sure to use all your past research and use the customer persona description you’ve created. The output should be detailed, visually structured, and strategic — suitable for use by copywriters, designers, marketers, and leadership. You are a world-class brand strategist and identity expert with a sharp understanding of emotional branding, positioning, and visual storytelling. Use the following structure and expand each section thoughtfully. Draw on buyer psychology, brand essence, tone of voice, and market fit. Every decision should feel intentional and tied back to the customer experience or brand mission. 

1. Brand Overview 
Brand Name: [PROVIDE 5-10 BRAND NAME OPTIONS TO CHOOSE FROM, with meaning/origin] 
Emotional & Linguistic Associations: What does the name evoke? 
Brand Essence: 1–2 sentence purpose statement. What is the core emotional promise or transformation the brand offers? 

2. Mission, Vision & Values 
Mission Statement: What the brand does and why it matters — action-driven, benefit-focused. 
Vision Statement: The long-term aspiration or impact. 
Core Values: 5 clear, emotionally resonant principles. Describe how each shows up in the business or product. 

3. Brand Personality 
Define 4–6 core traits. Include tone explanations (e.g., “Refined but Approachable”). For each trait, explain how it’s expressed across touchpoints like visuals, packaging, and content. 

4. Tone of Voice 
What does the brand sound like when it speaks? Include 3–4 tone attributes (e.g., Warm, Smart, Empowering, Calm). Include example phrases or slogans that demonstrate how the brand sounds in actual copy. 

5. Target Audience & Positioning 
Describe the primary and secondary audiences. Include demographic, psychographic, and emotional drivers. Clarify the category positioning — how this brand is different from competitors and what unique benefit it offers. 

6. Visual Identity 
6.1 Logo & Wordmark Conceptual meaning behind the logo. Usage guidelines: Primary, secondary, icon, color inversion. 
6.2 Color Palette Include 1–2 primary colors, 1–2 secondary accent colors, and neutrals. For each color, describe its emotional connotation and strategic purpose (e.g., "trust," "luxury," "calm"). 
6.3 Typography Headline font: style, tone, feel Body font: clarity, accessibility Usage guidance: hierarchy, tone expression, formatting tips 
6.4 Imagery & Photography Mood and style: lighting, color temperature, subject expressions Composition guidance (minimalist, editorial, lifestyle, etc.) Inclusivity considerations 

7. Brand Messaging & Taglines 
List 4–6 tagline options that reflect the brand’s core promise and emotional benefit. Optionally include use cases or taglines by audience segment. 

8. Brand Applications 
Packaging Design: Aesthetic direction, materials, layout, visual focus Website & Social: Layout, tone, CTA style, content categories Ads/Collateral: Design principles, image tone, copy style Influencer or community content: How to align creators with the brand's feel 

9. Do’s & Don’ts 
Give specific creative and tone guidelines to stay consistent Include visual and copy examples of what not to do 

10. Future-Proofing the Brand 
How the identity can flex for different audiences or new product lines Which elements are core and must remain consistent across all future iteration"

## Step 6: Tone of Voice
**Prompt to use:**
"You are a senior brand and e-commerce copy strategist.
Based on all the information you have on our brand (including the chosen Brand Name: [INSERT CHOSEN BRAND NAME]), create a comprehensive Tone of Voice guideline document for our copywriters.

Your goal:
Deliver a clear, practical document that helps any copywriter consistently write in our brand’s voice across all touchpoints (website, product pages, ads, emails, social media, customer service, etc.).

Requirements for the document:

1. Overview & Context
- Start with a short summary of: Who we are as a brand, What we sell, Our core brand promise and positioning, Our main target audience(s).
- Keep this section concise but clear.

2. Brand Personality & Voice Pillars
- Define the brand personality in 3–5 clear traits.
- For each trait: Explain what it means in practice for our writing, Describe how it should feel to the reader, Add 2–3 example phrases or short sentences that match this trait.

3. Core Tone of Voice Principles
- Describe 4–7 core principles that define how we speak (e.g. direct and clear, positive, customer-first).
- For each principle, include: A short explanation, Do’s: what copywriters should do, Don’ts: what they should avoid, 1–2 “bad” vs “better” example sentence pairs.

4. Language & Style Guidelines
- Clarify the basics of style and language, such as: Formal vs informal tone, Sentence length and structure, Use of questions/CTAs/storytelling, Rules about punctuation/capitalization/numbers.
- Add concrete examples for each rule.

5. Channel-Specific Guidelines (for E-commerce)
Based on the brand information, define how the tone of voice shows up in different channels:
- Homepage & landing pages
- Category pages
- Product detail pages
- Performance ads (Social, Display, Search)
- Email flows & newsletters
- Organic social posts
- Customer service / support communication

6. For each channel, explain:
- The main goal of the copy in that channel
- How the tone should adapt (if at all)
- Example sentences or short examples relevant to that channel.

7. Do’s & Don’ts Overview
- Create a clear, scannable list of Do’s and Don’ts.
- Illustrate with short example sentences.

8. Examples Library
- Provide multiple “Before vs After” examples where “Before” is generic/off-brand and “After” is fully aligned.
- Cover different use cases: product description, banner headline, call-to-action, email subject line, social post, etc.

9. Brand-Specific Vocabulary
- Create a mini glossary of: Important brand terms and phrases we often use, Phrases or concepts we avoid.
- Explain briefly why each term is important or forbidden.

10. Copywriter Checklist
- End with a short, practical checklist copywriters can use before publishing: 8–12 yes/no questions (e.g. “Does this sound like [brand name]?”, “Is the benefit for the customer crystal clear?”).

Style & Format Instructions:
- Write the entire document in a clear, structured format with headings, subheadings, bullet points, and examples.
- Make the document practical and easy to use for day-to-day writing.
- Use the main language of the brand materials I provide for all example sentences.
- When something is important, highlight it clearly in the text.
If anything in the brand information is unclear or contradictory, choose the most consistent interpretation and note your assumption briefly in the document."

## Step 7: Project Instructions Prompt
**Prompt to use:**
"You are a master AI project architect. Based on all the documentation we have just created (Product Explanation, Market Research, Customer Avatars, Deep Dive, Brand Guidelines, and Tone of Voice), write a 'super sick', highly detailed, and comprehensive prompt that I can use as my 'Project Instructions' in Manus (or any other AI agent).

This project instructions prompt must explicitly command the AI to:
1. ALWAYS use the references from the generated documentation.
2. ALWAYS write specifically for the defined customer persona. If there are multiple personas, the AI must ALWAYS ask the user which persona to focus on for a specific task, or if it should be kept general.
3. ALWAYS apply the exact Tone of Voice defined in the documentation for any copywriting.
4. ALWAYS adhere to the Brand Guidelines (visual identity, personality, etc.) whenever creating designs or visual assets.

Format the output as a ready-to-copy-paste prompt block. 

After generating the prompt, you must add the following exact text at the very end of your response to the user:
'Je kunt al deze documentatie nu als project aanmaken met deze instructies. Je bent nu helemaal ready to go voor de perfecte output. Heel veel succes!
Gr, Joshua & Team DSA'"
